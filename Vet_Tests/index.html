RVT Anatomy & Physiology Study Guide

Neurological Logic & Functional Mnemonics
D.A.V.E. LogicPathway through dorsal and ventral horn for impulses: **D**orsal = **A**fferent; **V**entral = **E**fferent.
P.A.D. (Meninges)Connective tissue layers surrounding brain/cord: **P**ia Mater (thinnest), **A**rachnoid (vascular/nutrients), **D**ura Mater (tough/fibrous).
HypothalamusLinks endocrine and nervous system by secreting/storing hormones in pituitary gland.
CNS vs PNSCNS: Brain and Spinal Cord. PNS: Cranial and Spinal Nerves.
Neurological Disease Mastery
IVDDHerniation of disk, commonly T10-12. Type I: Acute (small breeds). Type II: Chronic (large breeds). Dachshunds, Beagles, Corgis, Frenchies prone.
Degenerative MyelopathyCNS disease caused by SOD1 gene mutation. Prone breeds: GSDs, Boxers, Ridgebacks.
SyringomyeliaFluid cavities in neck cord. Sign: Phantom scratching. Breed: Cavalier King Charles.
Renal / Urinary (RAAS System)
RAA System GoalRegulate/Increase blood pressure. Low renal blood volume triggers **Renin** -> Angiotensin split -> **Aldosterone** -> Sodium/Water reabsorption.
Glomerular FiltrationHappens in the **Renal Corpuscle** (Glomerulus and Bowman's capsule).
Urinary AnatomyUreters move urine by **Peristalsis**. Bladder has transitional epithelium and sphincter muscles.
